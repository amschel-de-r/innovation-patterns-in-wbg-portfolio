# HR-07 — Innovation Taxonomy Validation

**Methodology step**: Step 7 — Develop the innovation taxonomy
**Corresponding prompts**: [step_07_taxonomy.md](../prompts/step_07_taxonomy.md)
**Reviewer qualifications**: See [reviewers.md](../reviewers.md)

---

## Purpose

Validate Python-generated provisional taxonomy assignments for a sample of projects in the innovative subset (High + Moderate tiers). Ensure category assignments are consistent with the operational definitions of the four innovation categories and supported by the underlying text. Resolve category conflicts and confirm multi-label assignments where appropriate.

## Four innovation categories

| Category | Operational definition |
|----------|----------------------|
| Operational / Institutional | Innovations in governance processes, public administration, service delivery mechanisms, or institutional arrangements |
| Technological | Innovations involving the adoption or adaptation of specific technologies |
| Collaborative | Innovations in partnership structures, community participation, or cross-sector coordination |
| Financial | Innovations in financing mechanisms, incentive structures, or resource mobilization |

## Reviewers

- N: 2 (paper authors)

## Materials provided to reviewers

- Provisional taxonomy assignments generated by Python keyword matching
- Lessons Learned text for each project in the innovative subset
- Operational definitions of the four categories (above)

## Decision rules

An assignment was **confirmed** if the underlying Lessons Learned text contained clear textual evidence supporting the category.

An assignment was **revised** if:
- The evidence in the text better matched a different category
- The LLM applied a category based on surface-level keyword presence rather than substantive innovation content

A **multi-label assignment** was made where the text contained clear evidence for more than one category.

**New categories**: No new categories were introduced during review. All assignments use the four pre-defined categories.

## Disagreement resolution

Discussion to consensus between the two author-reviewers. No formal third reviewer.

## Sample

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Total N reviewed | ~30–50 projects |
| Source pool | Innovative subset (High + Moderate tiers) |
| Selection method | Mixed: random draw, purposive (projects in reviewer's domain of expertise), and targeted (borderline or multi-label cases) |
