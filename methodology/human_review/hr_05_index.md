# HR-05 — Innovation Index Validation

**Methodology step**: Step 5 — Human validation
**Corresponding prompts**: None (no AI involvement in this review task)
**Reviewer qualifications**: See [reviewers.md](../reviewers.md)

---

## Purpose

Validate the automated Innovation Index by independently assessing whether project Lessons Learned texts contain substantive references to innovation, and whether the automated tier assignment is appropriate.

## Reviewers

- N: 2 primary reviewers (in-team members); a third reviewer independently examined a sample of Low tier projects
- Blinded: No

## Materials provided to reviewers

- Lessons Learned text for each sampled project
- No separate codebook was provided; reviewers drew on their existing domain experience and familiarity with the projects

## Sample

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Source pool | All tiers (High, Moderate, Low) |
| High tier | ~10% sampled; reviewers began with projects they were already familiar with |
| Moderate tier | Borderline cases prioritized |
| Low tier | Sample examined by a third reviewer |

## Coding task

For each project, reviewers assessed:

- Does the Lessons Learned text contain a substantive reference to innovation? (Yes / No / Borderline)
- (If project known) Was the project, to the best of your knowledge, innovative for its time? (Yes / No / Borderline)
- Is the automated tier assignment appropriate? (Yes / No / Borderline)

**Coding criterion**: Reviewers applied a broad definition of innovation — projects were flagged as innovative even where the innovation was primarily a pilot informing future lending rather than central to the project's own outcomes.

## Disagreement resolution

Disagreements were resolved through discussion between the two primary reviewers, with attention to whether keyword matches reflected genuine innovation content rather than generic project language.
